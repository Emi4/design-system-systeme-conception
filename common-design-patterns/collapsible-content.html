Collapsible content: Alpha design pattern

  • Last updated: Aug 28, 2019
  • Progress: Alpha - draft
  • Design type: Component
  • Lead: Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat

Update the guidance on collapsible content to allow for another use: force choices when answers are mutually exclusive.

The collapsible content pattern allows secondary content to be hidden until requested. The pattern consists of a trigger link that shows or hides the collapsible content.

Research

Testing
Use of collapsibe content to incite peope to choose between options was tested successfully during 2 optimization projects done with content from the Canada Revenue Agency

When to use

Colapsible content can be used in 2 situtions: forcing choice when the answers are mutually exclusive and presenting seondary information.

Choice between mutually exclusive answers

Use this component when people need to get different answers based on their situation, and these answers are mutually exclusive.

Presenting secondary information

Use to prevent secondary content (that must still appear on the page) from getting in the way of the primary task.

Use for content that is clearly of a secondary nature (for example, when only applicable to a small minority of your audience, or additional details that are beyond the core purpose of the page).

What to avoid

Don't use this component for primary content that applies for the majority of people who get to this page.

Don't use this component if you need peope to answer several questions to get their specific answer. In these cases, use interactive questions instead.

Implementation and example

Use only the WET Details/Summary component for this pattern

Choice between mutually exclusive answers

Determine if you are in a shared custody situation

Does the child spend their time:

about equally between both parents

If the child shares their time about equally between the parents, then both parents are considered to have shared custody of that child:

  • each parent must apply for Canada child benefit separately
  • the CRA will calculate the amount each parent will get separately, using their respective family income
  • each parent will get exactly 50% of what they would have gotten if they had sole custody of the child, calculated based on their own income
  • the CRA will not split the amount using other percentages, or give the full amount to one of the parents
mostly with you

If the child lives with you most of the time, you are considered to have primary custody for the purposes of the Canada child benefit:

  • only you can apply for Canada child benefit
  • the CRA will calculate your payment based on your family net income (not taking into account the other parent's income)
  • the CRA will not split the amount between you and the other parent
mostly with the other parent

If the child lives with the other parent most of the time, the other parent is considered to have primary custody for the purposes of the Canada child benefit:

  • only the other parent can apply for Canada child benefit
  • the CRA will calculate their payment based on their family net income (not taking into account your income)
  • the CRA will not split the amount between you and the other parent
mostly with the other parent, but temporarily mostly with you

Changes in custody can sometimes be temporary. This can happen, for example, if the child usually lives with one parent, but goes to live with the other parent for the summer.

In these cases, the parent that gets custody on a temporary basis can apply to get Canada child benefit payments for that period. When the child returns to live with the other parent, that parent needs to reapply to start getting the payments again.
