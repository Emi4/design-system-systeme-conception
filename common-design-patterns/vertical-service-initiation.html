Vertical service initiation template: Alpha design pattern

  • Last updated: Aug 13, 2019
  • Progress: Alpha - draft
  • Design type: Template
  • Lead: Canada Revenue Agency

Service initiation pages focus on helping Canada.ca visitors start a service or task. The set of pages should help prepare visitors for the documents or forms they will need to fill out in their circumstances and the questions they are likely to have after they apply for the service.

While many services involve transactions or an exchange of information between people and program administrators through forms, many services provided to Canadians by the government are non-transactional. For example, providing information about the weather, nutrition or product safety recalls all provide outcomes for people as part of service delivery.

Service initiation pages:

  • Support a consistent approach to task initiation and completion, including ordering by steps where required
  • Provide people with the information they need before initiating the task, offer alternate methods, where applicable, of completing a service; including online, in-person or by mail
  • Ensure that experienced clients (those who have previously used a particular service) can quickly initiate a task and choose whether or not to review all of the supporting information offered

When to use

  • Use for service overview and delivery over all available channels, such as phone, email, mail, etc.
  • Use to start tasks that support services (as defined under the Policy on Service)
  • Use to start tasks that have prerequisites to satisfy before starting the task (such as eligibility criteria or having documents or information in hand)
